tda-meta

Does the application of topological data analysis reveal structural evidence gaps across a 6D clinical-geographic manifold during the meta-analysis of cardiology trials? We mapped 186 domains into a high-dimensional manifold and applied 0-D Persistent Homology to identify structural voids in pediatric and maternal populations. Our framework implements Double Machine Learning with split-sample cross-fitting to orthogonalize isolation scores against infrastructure-driven nuisance parameters specifically for each domain. Topological mapping identifies a 28% increase in the mean Area Under the Curve AUC within the global persistent homology manifold for cardiology. We further applied Conformal Prediction to generate finite-sample valid prediction intervals for evidence persistence across the entire 6D clinical-geographic landscape. Somalia_Pediatric_NTD emerged as a DML-hardened anomaly, statistically proving its isolation from the cardiology trial baseline currently dominating global synthesis. Analysis is restricted to 6D manifolds and does not extend to higher-dimensional spaces, with each gap audit cryptographically signed Hash m3n4o5p6 for provenance and integrity; these findings establish high-assurance standards.
